Intersection closed finite relations

This idea is a continuation of the finite functions

This idea was motivated by trying to intersect the finite functions, to get new ones. And also a better way to develop software, by intersecting simple functions, to get a complex one.

What this algebra needs, is to be closed by intersection, that means if we intersect two elements of this algebra it needs to get an element of the algebra.

The following intersections cannot be expressed by the finite functions:

pp = p1 ∩ p2

⊥ = i1 ∩ i2

so we will introduce it to the algebra.

The new algebra will look like (IntFinRel; _._, <_,_>, [_,_], id, p1, p2, pp, i1, i2, dr, ⊥)

pp

This is a partial function that can be expressed by the relation {(a, (a, a)) | a ∈ A}, and can be thought as the converse of < id, id > or the special projection that forces the two elements to be the same

Has the property of:

pp . < R, S > = R ∩ S

⊥

This relation is called bottom and is the empty relation

Has the property of:

R . ⊥ = ⊥ = ⊥ . R

Classes of this algebra

(1) [ _, _ ] . dr . < _, _ >
(2) i* . < _, _ >
(3) i* . dr . < _, _ >
(4) [ _, _ ] . p*
(5) [ _, _ ] . dr . p*
(6) i* . p*
(7) i* . dr . p*
(8) ⊥

Proof that is closed to intersection

To prove this, we need to show that for every intersection between IntFinRel we can represent without ∩, and we can do it by "implementing" ∩.
